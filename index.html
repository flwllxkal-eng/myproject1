The Golden Rules of Strong Passwords

The first step to securing your online accounts is using a strong password. Here are the 'golden rules' recommended by security experts.

  • Length is Strength: Aim for at least 12 characters. The longer a password is, the exponentially harder it becomes to crack using brute-force attacks.
  • Complexity is Key: Always use a mix of uppercase letters (A-Z), lowercase letters (a-z), numbers (0-9), and special characters (!, @, #, etc.). A complex combination is harder to guess.
  • Uniqueness is Vital: Use a different, unique password for every single one of your accounts. If one service is breached, your other accounts will remain safe.
  • Avoid Personal Information: Never use easily guessable information like your name, birthday, phone number, or common phrases like 'password123'.
